Week 9

Learning outcomes

  • Analyse Meta campaign structure by distinguishing between campaign, ad set and ad, and explain how each level supports the overall objective.
  • Evaluate audience targeting by comparing Core, Custom and Lookalike Audiences, and identify when each is most useful.
  • Apply budgeting and bidding logic using CPM, CPC and CPA frameworks in line with awareness, traffic and conversion goals.
  • Design more effective ad creative by matching format choice to objective, buyer persona and placement context, using a clear CTA.
These outcomes intentionally combine platform mechanics with marketing judgement. Keep returning to the question: what business problem is this setting helping solve?
Campaign structure

Meta campaign structure

Campaign
The goal level
Sets the campaign objective and tells Meta what success should look like. One campaign should have one clear job.
↓
Ad set
The delivery level
Controls audience, placements, budget, schedule and bidding choices. This is where you shape who sees the campaign and how spend is allocated.
↓
Ad
The creative level
The actual message people see: visual, copy, headline, CTA and format. Multiple ads can sit in one ad set for testing.
Useful shorthand: campaign = why, ad set = who/where/how much, ad = what people actually experience.
Students often collapse ad set and ad together. Stress that the ad set is the delivery logic; the ad is the creative execution.
Objectives

Meta’s six campaign objectives

Awareness
Optimise for recall, reach or attention when the job is visibility rather than immediate action.
Traffic
Send people to a destination such as a website, app or landing page when visits matter most.
Engagement
Optimise for interactions such as post engagement, video views, messages or other on-platform actions.
Leads
Generate lead form submissions, calls or messages when qualification matters more than raw reach.
App promotion
Drive app installs and in-app actions when the app is the product or a critical touchpoint.
Sales
Optimise toward purchases and conversion value when the objective is revenue, not just attention.
Pro tip: objective choice is not cosmetic. It changes who Meta tries to find and what the delivery system optimises for.
Explain that Meta simplified the legacy objective structure into six top-level options. This is the current language students should use in class and in assessments.
Ad set decisions

What the ad set controls

Audience targeting
Age, location, interests, behaviours and first-party audience inputs all sit at ad set level.
Placements
Meta can run ads across Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, WhatsApp and Meta Audience Network, often via Advantage+ placements.
Budget and bidding
This is where you define spend, schedule and the bidding approach that best fits the campaign goal.
If the goal is...Likely ad set emphasis
AwarenessBroader targeting, scalable placements, visibility-focused budgeting and simpler creative testing.
TrafficLink-click or landing-page-visit logic, tighter audience hypotheses and stronger CTA discipline.
SalesConversion-ready audiences, data quality, event tracking and tighter efficiency expectations.
Students should see the ad set as the engine room of campaign delivery. The ad set is where strategy becomes operational.
Targeting

Core, Custom and Lookalike Audiences

Core Audiences
Built from Meta’s platform data such as age, location, interests, behaviours, language and device use. Best when you are testing reach with broad but relevant hypotheses.
Custom Audiences
Built from your own data such as website visitors, customer files, app users or prior engagement. Best for retargeting and warming up people who already know the brand.
Lookalike Audiences
Built from a source audience so Meta can find new people similar to your existing high-value users. Best for scaling once a strong source audience exists.
Simple rule: Core for testing, Custom for retargeting, Lookalike for scaling.
Keep the logic commercial. Core is often the starting point, Custom is usually the warmest audience, and Lookalike is the bridge between known value and new reach.
Budgeting and bidding

A practical teaching framework: CPM, CPC and CPA

CPM
Cost per thousand impressions. Best for awareness or reach when visibility is more important than immediate clicks or purchases.
CPC
Cost per click. Useful for traffic campaigns where the key question is how efficiently you can get visits to a landing page or site.
CPA
Cost per acquisition or action. Most useful when the campaign is conversion-focused and event tracking is set up reliably.
CPM, CPC and CPA are your working framework for this course. In practice, Meta expresses these choices through performance goals and bid strategy options inside Ads Manager — the underlying logic is the same.

Creative

Choosing ad formats by objective

ObjectiveRecommended formatsWhy it fits
AwarenessImage ads, short video, Stories, ReelsFast attention, broad reach and memorable brand cues.
TrafficImage or video with strong link CTA, carouselMakes the next action clear and gives users a reason to click through.
LeadsImage or video with lead form, message-driven unitsReduces friction when the goal is contact capture or qualification.
SalesCarousel, collection, product-led videoLets users evaluate product value quickly and supports lower-funnel intent.
App promotionDemo video, Stories/Reels with clear CTAShows the product experience and pushes the install action directly.
Avoid dated language like “swipe up.” Use a clear, specific CTA that tells your audience exactly what to do next. A format is only strong if it fits the campaign job.
Match creative to user intent. Remind students that a format is not good or bad in itself; it is only strong if it fits the campaign job.
Practice 1

Build the campaign structure

Scenario
Hnry wants to use Meta Ads for brand building in the UK and to increase users of its Mileage Tracker app in New Zealand. The brand wants to target two buyer personas and test the channel with a $5,000 budget.
Campaign + ad set decisions
  • How many campaigns would you recommend?
  • Which objective should the campaign use?
  • How many ad sets would you build, and why?
  • Which placements would you use?
  • How would you target each ad set?
Budget + bidding decisions
  • How would you split the budget across ad sets?
  • Which bidding model is most defensible?
  • How many ads would sit inside each ad set?
  • Which formats are best suited to the brief?
Let students work in pairs. The expected logic is usually one awareness campaign, at least two ad sets to reflect personas, and multiple ads per ad set for creative comparison.
Practice 2

Estimate impressions for a UK awareness campaign

Assumptions: budget = $5,000, one campaign, awareness objective, and CPM is the chosen bidding logic because the goal is brand visibility.
  • Research a current Meta CPM benchmark for a relevant category such as tech or consumer electronics.
  • Calculate predicted impressions using: Impressions = (Budget ÷ CPM) × 1,000.
  • Decide how success would be judged: impressions alone, reach quality, frequency, lift or downstream behaviour?
Benchmark rule: students should research current estimates before answering. CPM assumptions should be sourced, not invented.
This exercise is about estimation discipline. Students must not pull a CPM number from memory without explaining where it came from and why it is relevant.
Practice 3

Compare CPC and CPA logic for a bottom-of-funnel campaign

Scenario: Hnry asks for a 30-day Meta campaign focused on purchases and ROAS. Budget = $5,000. You must compare a CPC route with a CPA route.
Research inputs
  • Current CPC benchmark
  • Current CTR benchmark
  • Current CPA benchmark
  • Use NZ or the most relevant ecommerce benchmark you can justify
Use these scenario assumptions
  • Click-to-purchase conversion rate = 4.5%
  • Average order value = $1,300
  • ROAS = Revenue ÷ Ad Spend
OptionCalculation path
CPC strategyClicks = Budget ÷ CPC → Conversions = Clicks × 4.5% → Revenue = Conversions × average order value → ROAS = Revenue ÷ Budget
CPA strategyConversions = Budget ÷ CPA → Revenue = Conversions × average order value → ROAS = Revenue ÷ Budget
The purpose here is to get students to show their commercial reasoning step by step. They should be able to explain why one route might be more appropriate given data maturity and campaign risk.
Session summary

What to take away from today

1. Objective choice matters
Meta optimises differently depending on whether the campaign job is awareness, traffic, leads, app promotion, engagement or sales.
2. Ad sets are strategy in action
Audience, placements, budget and bidding choices sit at ad set level and materially shape results.
3. Creative is not separate from strategy
The best format is the one that fits the objective, audience intent and next-step behaviour.
If you can justify why this objective, why this audience, why this bid logic and why this format, you are thinking like a marketer rather than a button-pusher.
